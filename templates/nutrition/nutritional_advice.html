Nutritional Advice

What are Calories?

Calories are a unit of measurement that tell us how much energy food and drinks provide. The body uses this energy to perform essential functions such as breathing, circulating blood, maintaining body temperature, and supporting physical activity.

Everyone requires a different number of calories depending on factors such as age, sex, height, weight, muscle mass, and activity level. Consuming more calories than the body needs over time may lead to weight gain, while consistently consuming fewer calories than required may result in weight loss.

It is important to remember that not all calories are equal. A balanced diet that includes nutrient-rich foods provides the body with essential vitamins, minerals, and other nutrients needed for good health.

What are Fats, Carbohydrates and Protein?

Carbohydrates

Carbohydrates are the body's primary source of energy. They are broken down into glucose, which fuels the brain, muscles, and other organs. Healthy carbohydrate sources include:

  • Whole grains
  • Fruit and vegetables
  • Beans and legumes
  • Oats and wholemeal bread

Choosing complex carbohydrates over highly processed sugary foods can help maintain steady energy levels throughout the day.

Fats

Fats provide a concentrated source of energy and help the body absorb vitamins A, D, E, and K. They also support brain function and hormone production.

Unsaturated fats are generally considered healthier choices and can be found in:

  • Olive oil
  • Avocados
  • Nuts and seeds
  • Oily fish such as salmon and mackerel

Saturated fats should be consumed in moderation, while trans fats should be limited whenever possible.

Protein

Protein is essential for building and repairing muscles, skin, organs, and other body tissues. It also plays a vital role in the production of enzymes and hormones.

Good sources of protein include:

  • Lean meat and poultry
  • Fish and seafood
  • Eggs
  • Dairy products
  • Beans, lentils, and chickpeas
  • Tofu and other soy products
